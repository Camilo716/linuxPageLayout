DISCOVERING GNU-LINUX

Why Linux?

Linux is a free and open-source operating system that runs on a wide variety of devices, from servers and personal computers to mobile and IoT devices. In addition to being secure, stable, and highly customizable, Linux has a wide variety of free and open-source tools and software applications that make it ideal for developers, IT professionals, and end-users looking for a reliable and secure alternative to proprietary operating systems. If you value freedom, privacy, and security, you should seriously consider using Linux on your devices.

Basic Concepts

Distribution

Terminal

files System

Linux is available in many different distributions, or "distros," each with its own set of features, software packages, and user interfaces. Some popular Linux distros include Ubuntu, Fedora, Debian, and CentOS. It's important to choose the right Linux distribution for your needs and to become familiar with its unique features and package management system. Different distributions may also have different levels of community support and documentation available, so it's important to consider these factors when choosing a Linux distribution.

The terminal or command line is an important tool in Linux that allows you to interact with the operating system in a textual way. Although Linux has a graphical user interface, the terminal can be faster and more powerful in certain tasks, especially in system administration and programming. It's important to know some basic terminal commands, such as ls, cd, cp, mv, mkdir, rm, and more

Everything is treated as a file, including hardware devices and directories. The filesystem in Linux is hierarchical, which means that files are organized into directories or folders. It's important to understand the structure of the filesystem in Linux, as this will help you navigate, copy, move, and manage files and directories effectively.
